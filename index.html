Curonian Spit

URL

unesco.org

Here, between forests and sand dunes, you can see two water horizons - the calm Curonian Lagoon on one side and the rippling waves of the Baltic Sea on the other. Unique natural area on the edge of Russia.

The Kaliningrad region does not end there. For travelers and researchers there is a number of things in the neighborhood - the westernmost point of Russia, the Baltic Spit and the German heritage of small seaside towns. The atmosphere of these places eliminates the fuss, dipping into the tranquility of nature and the smell of steel, cool sea.

Kola Peninsula

URL

russiadiscovery.com

Almost the entire peninsula is located beyond the Arctic Circle. tWith taiga to the south and the Arctic Ocean to the north.

Perhaps you watched films by Zvyagintsev or even heard the story of the Arctic festival in Teriberka. If so, your dream of seeing the Northern Lights starts with a ticket to Murmansk.

Altai

URL

visitrussia.com

Altai is one of the most beautiful places in Russia. First of all, because of the mountains: if you drive along the ridge, you will see slopes strewn with pine trees, mountain rivers and lakes. And if you open the windows in the car, you can get acquainted with the invisible miracle of these places - the mountain air.

The climate in Altai is temperate, so it is best to go in summer. This way you will see everything - the variety of local flora and fauna. Moose roam the forests of Altai, eagles fly over the ridges, and roe deer graze on the plains. And the famous manuls are also inhabitants of the Altai Territory.

Winter Baikal

URL

lakebaikal.com

Everyone knows Baikal as the largest lake in the world. Many also know it is the largest source of fresh water and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

Baikal is also an ideal place for skijoring competitions. This is a sport where a skier ties himself to a motorcycle and the tandem tries to reach the highest possible speed on the ice. In March 2019 at the Baikal Mile festival a world record was set - 197.011 km/h.

Karelia

URL

thetribune.com

Siberia does not end in the Urals, it ends in Karelia. The forests of the Karelian national parks, due to impenetrable swamps, have never beed touched by an ax. Some pines are more than half a millennium old. Touch a living creature that saw the sun before Ivan the Terrible saw it. In the virgin forest for a hundred kilometers you will not find a man-made path. And on rare paths trees are marked with bear claws. That is how they let everyone know who's boss.
